Is BDCMail secure?

Yes...mostly.

The username and password are sent to the server without SSL encryption. That means that they are not encrypted in anyway. There is a slight chance that a h4xor could get your user/pass by inspecting the packets but it really is doubtful. If any one would like to donate some money so that I can get this on its own domain and get the secure connection, email me at jeremy@carbauja.com. I estimate $238.80 for two years of hosting and $95 per year for an SSL certificate.

BDCMail does use cookies to store your username and password. These cookies persist until you logout or close all open windows of your browser. In the near future I will add one of those "remember me" settings.

NOTE: BDCMail does not log any information what-so-ever. Your username/password is not being logged or saved by the program in any manner. No email information is stored on my server.

The email message is a garbled mess when I view it. You suck!

Yes...mostly.

Email messages today often come in muti-part messages. There will be an attachment, HTML, and just plain text. Over the next few weeks I am going to start to deal with these different types.

When an attachment comes in, it will be placed as a link at the bottom of the mail message. As of now, the attachment loads as garbled characters right in the message view frame.

Also, there will be links when HTML is included in the email message. The plain-text message will be shown but there will be a link that, when clicked, will open up a new window for the viewing of the HTML stuffs.
